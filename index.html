Spaghetti

Spaghetti's history traces back to Arab traders introducing dried pasta ("itriyya") to Sicily in the 8th century, though ancient noodle-like foods existed. Sicily became a pasta hub, exporting long pasta strands by the 12th century, with the name "spaghetti" (little cords) appearing in 1874. While initially eaten with butter/cheese, the iconic pairing with tomatoes emerged in the 19th century after tomatoes became widely accepted, and mass production and global fame followed in the 19th and 20th centuries. Steak

The history of steak traces back to ancient grilling, but the term and modern concept emerged in 15th-century Europe, with Scandinavian "steik" (roast) and Italian "bistecca" (beefsteak) popularizing thick cuts from hindquarters, especially in Florence with its Bistecca Fiorentina; it became a staple in America during the 19th-century cattle boom, leading to the rise of steakhouses and celebrated as a cultural icon for celebrations, from ancient bonfires to modern dining.
